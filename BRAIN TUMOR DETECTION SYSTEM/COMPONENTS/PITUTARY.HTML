Pituitary Tumor Signs & Symptoms

Understanding the various ways pituitary tumors can affect your body and health. Not all pituitary tumors cause symptoms, but when they do, they can manifest in several distinct ways.

Symptoms from Larger Tumors

Pituitary tumors that grow large enough can press on nearby brain structures, causing:

Vision and Neurological Issues

  • Vision problems (loss of peripheral vision)
  • Headaches
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Behavior or personality changes
  • Confusion
  • Seizures

Hormone Deficiency Symptoms

  • Feeling tired or weak
  • Loss of body hair
  • Loss of interest in sex
  • Irregular menstrual periods or loss of periods
  • Erectile dysfunction
  • Weight gain
  • Feeling cold
  • Constipation
  • Depression

Large tumors may cause a shortage of vasopressin, leading to excessive urination, extreme thirst, and potential dehydration. This is a serious condition requiring immediate medical attention.

üö® Pituitary Apoplexy

A medical emergency where bleeding or blocked blood vessels reduce blood supply to the pituitary. Symptoms develop quickly and require immediate treatment.

Growth Hormone Excess

In Children & Teens (Gigantism)

  • Very tall stature
  • Very large hands and feet
  • Thickening of facial features
  • Joint problems
  • Excessive sweating

In Adults (Acromegaly)

  • Swollen hands and feet
  • Changes in facial appearance
  • Wider spacing between teeth
  • Joint pain
  • Deeper voice
  • Excessive sweating and body odor
  • Carpal tunnel syndrome
  • Muscle weakness
  • Diabetes
  • High blood pressure
  • Heart disease
  • Arthritis
  • Colon polyps

üí° Important Note

Many acromegaly changes occur slowly. People often don't notice them until comparing with old photos or trying on items they haven't worn in years.

ACTH Excess (Cushing's Disease)

High ACTH levels cause excess cortisol production, leading to:

Physical Changes

  • Weight gain (especially around midsection and back)
  • Purple stretch marks
  • Easy bruising
  • Weakened bones (osteoporosis)
  • High blood sugar or diabetes
  • High blood pressure
  • Muscle weakness

Emotional & Mental Changes

  • Irritability
  • Depression
  • Mood swings

In Women

  • Irregular or absent menstrual periods
  • Extra hair growth on face and body

üë∂ Children with Cushing's Disease

May experience growth problems and difficulties with school performance in addition to adult symptoms.

Prolactin Excess (Prolactinomas)

Most common in young women and older men:

In Women

  • Irregular or absent menstrual periods
  • Milky discharge from breasts
  • Loss of interest in sex
  • Infertility
  • Hot flashes
  • Vaginal dryness

In Men

  • Erectile dysfunction
  • Decreased interest in sex
  • Infertility
  • Rarely: enlarged breasts and milky discharge

üîç Detection Note

In females without periods (girls before puberty, postmenopausal women), prolactinomas might go unnoticed until they grow large enough to cause headaches and vision problems.

TSH Excess (Rare)

These tumors cause hyperthyroidism symptoms:

  • Weight loss
  • Rapid or irregular heartbeat
  • Nervousness or irritability
  • Frequent bowel movements
  • Sweating
  • Tremors
  • Sleep problems

Gonadotropin-Secreting Tumors

Most don't produce enough hormones to cause symptoms and behave like non-functional tumors:

Common Symptoms

  • Headaches
  • Vision problems
  • Lower sex hormone levels
  • Irregular periods (women)
  • Erectile dysfunction (men)

When Excess Hormones Are Produced

  • Usually no symptoms in middle-aged adults
  • Irregular periods in younger women
  • Early puberty signs in girls
