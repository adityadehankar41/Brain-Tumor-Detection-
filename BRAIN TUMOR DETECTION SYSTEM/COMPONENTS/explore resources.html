Brain Tumor Resources

Comprehensive support and information for patients, families, and caregivers

Finding Support and Information

Being diagnosed with a brain tumor or supporting someone who has been can be overwhelming. This resource guide aims to provide reliable information about organizations, support services, treatment options, and financial assistance available to those affected by brain tumors.

Medical Information and Research

National Brain Tumor Society (NBTS)

Research & Education

The largest nonprofit organization dedicated to the brain tumor community in the United States. NBTS offers comprehensive information about brain tumor types, treatments, research initiatives, and clinical trials.

Website: braintumor.org

American Brain Tumor Association (ABTA)

Research & Support

Founded in 1973, ABTA provides educational resources, treatment information, and support services for patients and caregivers affected by brain tumors. Their website includes a database of treatment centers.

Website: abta.org

National Cancer Institute (NCI)

Government Research

Part of the National Institutes of Health, NCI offers detailed information about brain tumor diagnosis, treatment options, clinical trials, and research advancements.

Website: cancer.gov/types/brain

Brain Tumor Foundation

Research & Support

Focuses on early detection, treatment options, and support for those affected by brain tumors. They also offer free brain scans in some locations.

Website: braintumorfoundation.org

Support Services

Cancer Support Community

Emotional Support

Offers free emotional support, education, and hope for people impacted by cancer, including online and in-person support groups specific to brain tumors.

Website: cancersupportcommunity.org

CancerCare

Counseling & Support

Provides free professional support services to anyone affected by cancer, including counseling, support groups, educational workshops, and financial assistance.

Website: cancercare.org

Local Support Groups

Community Support

Many hospitals and community centers host in-person support groups for brain tumor patients and their families. Ask your healthcare provider about local options or search the ABTA support group database.

Financial Assistance

Patient Advocate Foundation

Financial Aid

Offers case management services and financial aid to patients who meet certain qualifications. They can help navigate insurance issues and medical debt.

Website: patientadvocate.org

CancerCare Financial Assistance Programs

Financial Aid

Provides limited financial assistance for cancer-related costs, including transportation, home care, and childcare.

Website: cancercare.org/financial

The Samfund

Financial Aid

Supports young adult cancer survivors with financial grants to help with medical bills, living expenses, and educational costs.

Website: thesamfund.org

Clinical Trials

ClinicalTrials.gov

Research Database

A database of privately and publicly funded clinical studies being conducted around the world. You can search specifically for brain tumor trials.

Website: clinicaltrials.gov

Brain Tumor Trials Collaborative (BTTC)

Research Network

A network of medical centers with the expertise and strong desire to participate in state-of-the-art clinical trials investigating new treatments for malignant brain tumors.

Website: btcollaborative.org

For Caregivers

Caregiver Action Network

Caregiver Support

Provides education, peer support, and resources specifically for family caregivers, including those caring for brain tumor patients.

Website: caregiveraction.org

Family Caregiver Alliance

Caregiver Support

Offers information, education, services, research, and advocacy for caregivers of those with chronic or disabling health conditions.

Website: caregiver.org

Need immediate assistance?

If you're facing a medical emergency, please call 911 or go to your nearest emergency room.

For non-emergency questions, the American Brain Tumor Association's CareLine can help: 800-886-ABTA (2282)
