About Brain Tumor Types
Glioma

Tumors that occur in the glial cells of the brain. They can be low-grade (slow-growing) or high-grade (fast-growing) and make up about 30% of all brain tumors.

Meningioma

Slow-growing tumors that form on the membranes (meninges) surrounding the brain and spinal cord. They account for about 37% of primary brain tumors.

Pituitary

Tumors that develop in the pituitary gland at the base of the brain. Most are benign but can affect hormone production and vision.
